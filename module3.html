Ingredient Deep-Dive

14 ingredients · 5 frameworks each: WHAT → WHY → HOW → WITHOUT IT → DOSE RATIONALE
Tier 1 — Primary Amino Acid Substrates (The Healing Engine)
Arg
L-Arginine
7 g per sachet — highest dose ingredient
Nitric Oxide precursor · Angiogenesis booster · Collagen scaffold driver
What It Is

A conditionally essential amino acid — normally synthesised in the body, but becomes deficient during illness, diabetes, and chronic wounds. The most important substrate for wound-site blood flow and collagen production.

Why It's Needed in Wound Patients

Diabetic patients have chronically impaired endothelial NO production. Chronic wound patients are arginine-depleted due to arginase I overexpression at wound sites. Without supplementation, the wound bed remains hypoxic and ischaemic.

How It Works — Two Parallel Pathways
L-Arginine → NOS enzyme → Nitric Oxide (NO) → Vasodilation → ↑ Blood flow to wound → ↑ O₂ & nutrient delivery
L-Arginine → Arginase I → Ornithine → Proline → Collagen synthesis ↑

Also activates immune cell (macrophage and T-cell) function and drives fibroblast proliferation in the proliferative phase.

Without It
  • Wound bed remains hypoxic
  • Angiogenesis fails to initiate
  • Collagen scaffold is weak
  • Granulation tissue does not form
  • Wound stays in inflammatory phase
Dose Rationale — Why 7g?

Studies by Barbul et al. (2002) and Cereda et al. (2015) used 7–14g daily. The 7g dose in Epithrive® matches the minimum effective therapeutic dose proven in clinical literature for wound healing, without exceeding safe limits for diabetic and elderly patients.

Key Evidence
Cereda 2015: 19% greater wound-area reduction Barbul 2002: ↑ collagen deposition in elderly (Annals of Surgery) Tatti 2012: DFU healing time 247→83 days CSR-ETH-2025-001: 72.4% reduction (India study)
Safety note: May enhance hypotensive effects of ACE inhibitors. May cause mild flushing/warmth in some patients — this is vasodilation, not an allergic reaction. Advise patients this is expected and transient.
Gln
L-Glutamine
7 g per sachet — immune fuel & gut integrity guardian
Primary immune cell fuel · Fibroblast activator · Gut barrier protector
What It Is

The most abundant amino acid in human plasma — conditionally essential under stress. Glutamine is the preferred fuel of immune cells, gut epithelial cells, and fibroblasts. Under wound/surgical stress, plasma levels fall 50–80%.

Why It's Needed in Wound Patients

Trauma, surgery, and chronic illness deplete glutamine. This leads to immune failure, increased infection susceptibility, gut barrier breakdown (bacterial translocation), and impaired fibroblast function — all of which stall wound healing.

How It Works — Three Mechanisms
L-Glutamine → Macrophage fuel → ↑ Phagocytosis → Infection control
L-Glutamine → Fibroblast proliferation → ↑ Collagen deposition → Faster granulation
L-Glutamine → Gut barrier integrity → ↓ Bacterial translocation → ↓ Systemic infection
Without It
  • Immune cells lack fuel → infection risk ↑
  • Fibroblasts cannot proliferate
  • Gut wall leaks → bacteria enter bloodstream
  • ICU patients: ↑ mortality, ↑ sepsis
  • Nitrogen balance becomes negative
Dose Rationale — Why 7g?

Clinical studies use 14–30g/day in ICU settings. The 7g oral dose in Epithrive® is calibrated for outpatient/home use, where high-dose parenteral glutamine is not feasible. Sufficient to restore depleted levels and support healing without renal burden.

Key Evidence
Wischmeyer 2003: 45% reduction in ICU infections Stehle 1989: ↑ nitrogen balance, ↓ infections Nutrients Meta-Analysis 2021: significant effect on IL-6, TNF-α, LOS
Safety note: Safe in most patients. Caution in severe hepatic failure (glutamine → ammonia). Monitor in CKD Stage 4–5.
Col
Marine Collagen Peptides
2.5 g per sachet — pre-hydrolysed for maximum bioavailability
ECM scaffold builder · Fibroblast activator · Tissue tensile strength provider
What It Is

Pre-hydrolysed collagen from marine (fish) sources, broken into dipeptides and tripeptides — primarily Gly-Pro-Hyp (glycine-proline-hydroxyproline). Collagen is the primary structural protein of all human tissue; it forms 70–80% of skin's dry weight and is the main ECM component in wounds.

Why It's Needed in Wound Patients

Chronic wounds are characterised by collagen degradation exceeding deposition. Diabetic patients have impaired fibroblast collagen synthesis. Marine peptides provide direct precursors for new ECM formation and signal fibroblasts to produce more endogenous collagen.

How It Works
Marine Collagen → Hydrolysed → Gly-Pro-Hyp peptides → PEPT1 transporter (intact absorption) → Peak plasma in 1 hour → Accumulates in wound matrix → Fibroblasts activated → New collagen fibres deposited

Why marine over bovine? Marine collagen has Type I collagen content identical to human skin, superior bioavailability (smaller molecular weight), and avoids religious/cultural restrictions on bovine sources.

Without It
  • ECM formation is slow and fragile
  • Granulation tissue has poor tensile strength
  • Wound re-opens after apparent closure
  • Epithelialization is delayed
Dose Rationale — Why 2.5g?

Effective wound healing studies use 2.5–10g/day of collagen peptides. Epithrive® uses 2.5g as the foundational dose; Arginine's conversion to Proline provides additional collagen substrate — they work together. Increasing collagen peptides alone without amino acid co-factors yields diminishing returns.

Key Evidence
Oesser 1999: Collagen peptides accumulate directly at wound sites Asserin 2015: ↑ dermal collagen density Bagheri 2019 (Burns): Hydrolysed collagen ↑ burn wound healing vs control
Allergy note: Contains marine (fish) source. Ask patients about fish allergy before recommending. Rare but documented.
HMB
Ca-HMB (Calcium β-Hydroxy-β-Methylbutyrate)
1.5 g per sachet — anti-catabolic agent
Muscle preservation · mTOR activator · Cell membrane stabiliser
What It Is

A naturally occurring metabolite of the essential amino acid Leucine. In the body, ~5% of dietary leucine is converted to HMB via the KIC-dioxygenase enzyme. HMB is the bioactive molecule responsible for Leucine's anti-catabolic effect. Ca-HMB (calcium salt) is the most bioavailable and clinically validated form — 97% oral bioavailability.

Why It's Needed in Wound Patients

Chronic wounds create a highly catabolic state — the body breaks down muscle protein for energy and immune substrates. This leads to sarcopenia, immobility, pressure injury risk, and impaired oxygen delivery to wound tissue. HMB interrupts this destructive cycle.

How It Works — Three Mechanisms
Ca-HMB → Inhibits ubiquitin-proteasome pathway → ↓ Muscle protein breakdown by 57%
Ca-HMB → Activates mTOR pathway → ↑ Protein synthesis → Preserves / rebuilds lean mass
Ca-HMB → HMB-CoA → Stabilises cell membranes → Supports structural repair

Clinical implication: A patient who retains muscle mass has better mobility, better pressure distribution, and better oxygen delivery — all of which accelerate wound closure.

Without It
  • Muscle breakdown continues unchecked
  • Patient becomes weaker and less mobile
  • Oxygen delivery to wound ↓
  • Pressure injury risk on other sites ↑
  • Recovery is longer and more difficult
Dose Rationale — Why 1.5g?

Nissen (1996) established 3g/day as the benchmark dose for maximal anti-catabolic effect. Epithrive® uses 1.5g as an effective dose for wound patients (less intensive physical stress than athletes). Combined with Arginine and Glutamine, the anti-catabolic effect is synergistic — each ingredient reduces catabolism through a different pathway.

Key Evidence
Nissen 1996: 57% reduction in muscle proteolysis Williams 2002 (Annals Surgery): 3.5-day ↓ hospital stay in trauma Kuhls 2007: HMB safe and effective in critically ill trauma patients
IP Note (India vs. Global formulation): Ca-HMB is present in the India formulation only. The global formulation (USA, UAE, Europe) replaces Ca-HMB with L-Proline (1.5g) + L-Lysine (1.5g) to avoid patent overlap. Do not reference this distinction unless directly asked.
Tier 2 — Vitamins (The Enzymatic Enablers)
C
Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
60 mg per sachet — fully absorbed at this dose
Collagen hydroxylation · Antioxidant · Immune enhancer
What It Is & Why Needed

An essential water-soluble vitamin. Chronically ill and hospitalised patients are almost universally deficient. Vitamin C is the rate-limiting cofactor for collagen synthesis — without it, the collagen triple helix cannot be stabilised regardless of how much Arginine or Collagen Peptides are present.

How It Works
Vitamin C → Prolyl/Lysyl hydroxylase cofactor → Collagen triple helix stabilised

Also neutralises reactive oxygen species (ROS) generated during inflammation — protecting healthy cells around the wound from oxidative damage. Supports neutrophil function and immune response.

Without It

Collagen produced is unstable and breaks down rapidly. This is why scurvy — Vitamin C deficiency — is characterised by wound breakdown and failure to heal. Even mild deficiency significantly impairs collagen formation.

Dose Note

60mg is fully absorbed (SVCT1 transporter saturates above 200mg). This is the optimal therapeutic dose — higher amounts are simply excreted. Calibrated for wound support without GI side effects.

A
Vitamin A (Retinol)
750 mcg per sachet
Epithelialization driver · Fibroblast differentiator · Immune modulator
How It Works & Why Needed

Converted to retinoic acid (active form) which directly regulates gene expression for epithelial cell differentiation. Vitamin A drives keratinocyte migration across the wound surface (re-epithelialization), promotes fibroblast differentiation, and modulates the inflammatory phase to prevent it from becoming chronic. Deficiency in elderly and diabetic patients is common.

Without It

Re-epithelialization is delayed. The wound surface remains open longer, increasing infection risk. Inflammatory phase is poorly regulated — wounds get stuck in pathological chronic inflammation.

Caution: Vitamin A is fat-soluble and accumulates. The 750mcg dose in Epithrive® is within safe daily limits. Caution in patients already on high-dose Vitamin A supplements. Supervised use in pregnancy recommended.
E
Vitamin E (Tocopherol)
7.5 mg per sachet
Primary lipid antioxidant · Cell membrane protector
How It Works

Stored in cell membranes. During the inflammatory phase, ROS (reactive oxygen species) attack cell membranes of healthy tissue surrounding the wound. Vitamin E is the primary fat-soluble antioxidant that neutralises these ROS before they cause collateral damage. Works synergistically with Vitamin C (which regenerates oxidised Vitamin E).

Without It

Healthy peri-wound tissue is damaged by oxidative stress during inflammation. The healing area effectively expands rather than contracts. Healing is slower and scarring can be worse.

B
B Vitamins — B3 (Niacinamide), B6 (Pyridoxine), B12 (Cobalamin)
B3: 10.5mg · B6: 1.43mg · B12: 1.65mcg
Cellular energy · DNA synthesis · Amino acid metabolism cofactors
Vitamin B3 (Niacinamide)

Precursor to NAD+ — the primary cellular energy currency. Wound healing is an extremely energy-intensive process: immune cell activation, fibroblast proliferation, protein synthesis, and angiogenesis all require NAD+. B3 ensures the metabolic machinery runs at full capacity.

Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)

Essential cofactor for amino acid metabolism. B6 directly enables the conversion and utilisation of both L-Arginine and L-Glutamine within healing tissue. Without adequate B6, the therapeutic amino acids in Epithrive® cannot be fully processed.

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Critical for DNA synthesis in rapidly dividing cells. During the proliferative phase, fibroblasts, keratinocytes, and immune cells divide rapidly — all requiring B12. Particularly important in elderly patients and those with diabetic neuropathy, where B12 deficiency is common and impairs nerve function in wound tissue.

Without Them
  • Energy-deprived healing cells → slow repair
  • Amino acids not metabolised efficiently
  • DNA synthesis errors in rapidly dividing cells
  • Neuropathic symptoms worsen in diabetics
Tier 3 — Minerals (The Structural & Enzymatic Support Layer)
Zn
Zinc
13 mg per sachet — targeted therapeutic dose
DNA polymerase cofactor · Epithelialization driver · MMP regulator
Why It's Critical

Zinc is a cofactor for over 300 enzymes in the human body. In wound healing, it serves three irreplaceable roles: (1) cofactor for DNA polymerase — enabling cell division in rapidly proliferating wound cells; (2) supports keratinocyte (skin cell) migration across the wound surface; (3) regulates matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) which control the breakdown and rebuilding of ECM during remodeling. Zinc deficiency is extremely common in diabetic, elderly, and chronically ill patients.

Without It
  • Cell division in wound area slows dramatically
  • Epithelialization is impaired
  • MMP dysregulation → collagen is broken down faster than rebuilt
  • Immune function is weakened
  • Wound stays open longer, infection risk ↑
Drug interaction: Zinc reduces absorption of tetracycline and quinolone antibiotics. Space Epithrive® 2–3 hours apart from these medications. Also applies to Calcium for levothyroxine interactions.
Cu
Copper
1.3 mg per sachet
Collagen cross-linking via lysyl oxidase · Connective tissue integrity
How It Works

Copper is an essential cofactor for lysyl oxidase — the enzyme that cross-links collagen and elastin fibres into a stable, tensile matrix. Without cross-linking, individual collagen fibres are laid down but remain structurally weak. The wound can appear to close but ruptures under minimal mechanical stress. Copper converts individual fibres into a robust, interconnected scaffold.

Without It

Collagen fibres are deposited but not cross-linked. The healed wound has poor tensile strength and high risk of dehiscence (wound re-opening). This is the "look healed but breaks open" phenomenon common in malnourished surgical patients.

Se
Selenium
30 mcg per sachet
Glutathione peroxidase cofactor · Primary antioxidant enzyme system
How It Works

Selenium is incorporated into selenoproteins, most importantly glutathione peroxidase (GPx) — the body's primary enzyme-based antioxidant system. While Vitamins C and E neutralise individual ROS, GPx systematically processes hydrogen peroxide and lipid peroxides into harmless water. Works alongside Vitamin E as the antioxidant defence system protecting the healing wound from oxidative damage.

Without It

Antioxidant defence is incomplete. Oxidative stress accumulates in wound tissue, damaging fibroblasts and immune cells. Particularly critical in diabetic patients who have chronically elevated oxidative stress.

Ca
Calcium
200 mg per sachet
Cell signalling · Enzyme activation · Structural support for HMB delivery
Role in Wound Healing

Calcium regulates intracellular signalling cascades that control cell proliferation, migration, and differentiation — all critical processes in wound repair. It is also required for cytokine secretion by immune cells and enzymatic activity across the healing cascade. Additionally, Ca-HMB (the HMB form used in Epithrive®) delivers HMB alongside calcium — the calcium salt form has superior bioavailability compared to free HMB acid.

Without It

Multiple enzyme cascades in wound healing are impaired. Keratinocyte differentiation is slowed. Ca-HMB cannot be efficiently delivered without the calcium carrier. Bone health (relevant for bedridden/pressure injury patients) is also supported.

Drug interaction: Calcium reduces absorption of levothyroxine (thyroid medication). Advise patients on levothyroxine to take Epithrive® at a different time of day (minimum 4 hours apart).

Doctor FAQs

Every question a doctor will ask — with calibrated, compliant answers
A — Product & Formulation Questions
1
What exactly is Epithrive® and how is it different from standard nutritional supplements like Ensure or Fresubin?
›

Answer: Epithrive® is a wound-specific nutritional formulation — not a general ONS. Standard supplements like Ensure or Fresubin are designed for caloric repletion in malnourished patients. They provide general protein, carbohydrates, and fats. What they do not provide is the wound-targeted combination that Epithrive® delivers:

  • 7g L-Arginine — a therapeutic dose for nitric oxide production and angiogenesis. Most ONS have <1g.
  • 7g L-Glutamine — for immune cell fuel and gut barrier integrity. Absent in most ONS.
  • Ca-HMB — anti-catabolic metabolite. Absent in all standard ONS.
  • 2.5g Marine Collagen Peptides — direct ECM precursors. Absent in standard ONS.
  • No added sugar — appropriate for diabetic patients who make up the majority of our wound patient population.

Think of it this way: an Ensure gives a patient fuel. Epithrive® gives the body the specific raw materials the wound biology actually needs.

2
Why is the Arginine dose 7g? Isn't that very high?
›

Answer: 7g is not excessive — it is the minimum effective therapeutic dose supported by clinical literature. Barbul et al. (2002, Annals of Surgery) used 14g/day and demonstrated significant collagen deposition improvement in elderly volunteers. The Cereda et al. studies on pressure ulcers used similar doses. The 7g in Epithrive® is the lower bound of what is clinically effective.

To put it in context: a healthy adult synthesises and metabolises approximately 20–30g of Arginine per day endogenously. In wound patients — especially diabetics — endothelial NO production is impaired and wound-site arginase activity is elevated, meaning local Arginine is rapidly consumed. 7g oral supplementation brings this depleted pool back to functional levels.

At 7g, the dose is well below the threshold associated with adverse effects. No added sugar and low osmolality minimise GI intolerance.

3
What is HMB and why is it in a wound product? I've only seen it in sports nutrition.
›

Answer: HMB (beta-hydroxy-beta-methylbutyrate) is indeed used in sports nutrition — but its mechanism is directly applicable to wound healing. HMB is a metabolite of the essential amino acid Leucine, and its primary action is inhibiting muscle protein breakdown via the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway.

In sports, this preserves and builds muscle mass. In wound patients, the value is different but equally important:

  • Chronic wounds create a highly catabolic state. Patients lose significant muscle mass, especially bedridden elderly patients.
  • Muscle loss impairs mobility — which worsens pressure redistribution and increases risk of new ulcers.
  • Muscle loss reduces oxygen delivery capacity — a hypoxic wound heals poorly.
  • HMB interrupts the catabolic cycle, preserving lean mass and supporting systemic recovery.

Williams et al. (2002, Annals of Surgery) showed a 3.5-day reduction in hospital stay with Arg+HMB+Gln supplementation in trauma patients. The mechanism works whether the patient is an athlete or a wound patient — catabolism is catabolism.

4
Why marine collagen specifically? Can't the body produce its own collagen?
›

Answer: The body can produce endogenous collagen — but in chronic wound patients, this production is severely impaired for three reasons: (1) the substrate supply (Arginine → Proline → Collagen) is depleted; (2) Vitamin C (the essential cofactor for collagen hydroxylation) is deficient; (3) fibroblast function is impaired by the inflammatory environment.

Marine Collagen Peptides bypass the endogenous synthesis bottleneck by providing pre-formed Gly-Pro-Hyp tripeptides that are absorbed intact and deposited directly at wound sites (Oesser 1999). They also act as signalling molecules — stimulating fibroblasts to produce additional endogenous collagen.

Why marine over bovine? Marine collagen is primarily Type I — the same type as human skin. It has a lower molecular weight than bovine collagen and superior oral bioavailability. It also avoids concerns around BSE and is acceptable across dietary restrictions in most Indian communities.

B — Clinical & Safety Questions
6
Is it safe for my diabetic patients? Doesn't it contain sugar?
›

Answer: Epithrive® contains no added sugar — this was a deliberate formulation decision given that the majority of chronic wound patients in India are diabetic. The orange flavour is derived from natural flavouring, not from sucrose or glucose addition.

The primary ingredients — L-Arginine, L-Glutamine, Marine Collagen Peptides, and Ca-HMB — are amino acids and amino acid metabolites. They do not directly raise blood glucose. Arginine can transiently stimulate insulin secretion (which is generally beneficial in wound patients), but at 7g/day this effect is minimal.

The product has low osmolality, which minimises GI discomfort — a common concern in diabetic patients with gastroparesis.

7
Can I use this in CKD patients? Many of my wound patients have renal compromise.
›

Answer: The answer depends on CKD stage:

  • CKD Stage 1–3 (eGFR >30): Epithrive® is safe at standard dosing. The amino acid load (14g total Arg + Gln) is within acceptable limits for Stage 1–3 patients. Monitor renal function if using long-term.
  • CKD Stage 4 (eGFR 15–30): Use with caution. Reduce to 1 sachet/day and monitor serum urea and creatinine. Clinical judgement required — the benefit of wound healing support may outweigh the renal amino acid burden in selected patients.
  • CKD Stage 5 / Dialysis: Not recommended without nephrology clearance. The amino acid load requires careful management in end-stage renal failure.

In practice, most wound patients — even those with diabetic nephropathy — fall into CKD Stage 1–3 where Epithrive® is appropriate. We recommend checking baseline creatinine and eGFR when starting.

Does Arginine at this dose cause any problems? I'm concerned about lysine/arginine balance and herpes reactivation.
›

Answer: You're thinking about the theoretical concern that high-dose Arginine supplementation could favour herpes simplex virus (HSV) replication, as HSV uses Arginine for replication while Lysine competitively inhibits this.

This is a legitimate consideration, but the clinical context matters: in patients who are immunocompetent and HSV-negative, or HSV-positive but currently in latency, this is not a clinically significant concern at 7g/day. The evidence for Arginine supplementation causing HSV reactivation comes largely from in vitro studies, not robust clinical trials.

However, as a precaution: avoid Epithrive® in patients with an active HSV outbreak. For patients with known recurrent oral or genital herpes, use clinical judgement — the wound healing benefit likely outweighs the theoretical risk in most cases, but this is a conversation to have with the patient.

My patient is on warfarin / blood thinners. Is Epithrive® safe?
›

Answer: The Vitamin E content (7.5mg) in Epithrive® is well below the dose at which clinically significant potentiation of anticoagulation occurs (>400mg/day). At 7.5mg, the interaction with warfarin is negligible.

However, as with any new supplement in a patient on anticoagulation, we recommend monitoring INR at the first follow-up after initiating Epithrive®. Standard practice is to flag any dietary change to the anticoagulation team. This is good clinical caution rather than a product-specific concern.

10
My patient is on tetracycline/ciprofloxacin for wound infection. Can they take Epithrive® simultaneously?
›

Answer: Not simultaneously — but with a simple timing adjustment. The Zinc in Epithrive® can chelate tetracycline and quinolone antibiotics (like ciprofloxacin) in the gut, reducing absorption of both. This is a class-level interaction, not Epithrive®-specific.

The solution is straightforward: take the antibiotic first on an empty stomach (as already recommended for these drugs), then wait 2–3 hours before taking Epithrive®. This eliminates the interaction. In practice, antibiotic doses are usually morning/evening; Epithrive® can be taken midday or at a different meal time. The wound healing benefit of Epithrive® is important enough to continue it alongside antibiotic therapy — just separate them in time.

11
Can elderly patients tolerate the product? Won't 7g Arginine cause nausea or GI upset?
›

Answer: GI tolerability has been addressed in the formulation design. Epithrive® has low osmolality, which minimises the GI effects commonly associated with high-dose amino acid supplements.

A small percentage of patients (<5%) may experience mild bloating, gas, or mild abdominal discomfort in the first 2–3 days. This is transient and resolves as the gut adapts. Some patients notice a mild flush or feeling of warmth — this is the Arginine-driven vasodilation working, not an adverse reaction. It is actually a sign the product is active.

For elderly patients with gastroparesis or sensitive GI tracts, the sachet can be dissolved in cool water and sipped slowly rather than consumed quickly. Starting with half a sachet for the first 2–3 days and building up is an option in particularly sensitive patients, though not routinely necessary.

Is it safe in post-surgical patients who are on parenteral nutrition or other IV support?
›

Answer: Yes, with monitoring of total amino acid intake. Epithrive® can be used as a bridge from IV nutritional support to oral nutrition, or alongside parenteral nutrition in patients who can tolerate oral intake. The key consideration is total nitrogen load — if a patient is already receiving high-dose IV amino acids, the additional 14g from Epithrive® should be factored into the total protein prescription.

In practice, as patients transition to oral feeding post-surgery, Epithrive® adds the wound-specific substrates (Arginine, Glutamine, Collagen, HMB) that are absent in standard post-surgical nutritional protocols. This is exactly the phase where wound nutrition support has the highest impact — immediately post-operatively when the wound biology is most active.

C — Evidence & Efficacy Questions
13
What is the clinical evidence for Epithrive® specifically? Is there an Indian study?
›

Answer: Yes. A prospective open-label comparative Indian study (CSR-ETH-2025-001) was conducted across diabetic foot ulcers, pressure injuries, and post-surgical wounds over 8–10 weeks. n=60 patients (30 Epithrive® + standard care vs. 30 standard care alone).

Primary finding: 72.4% mean wound area reduction in the Epithrive® arm versus 52.1% in the control arm at Week 8. p=0.004 — statistically significant. This represents a 39% relative improvement in healing rate.

Principal Investigator: Dr. Praful Bhanegaonkar, MS Ortho. Co-Investigator: Dr. Gauri Jahagirdar, MD IDCCM. The full Clinical Study Report is available on request.

Compliant framing: Say "the study demonstrated 72.4% wound area reduction in the Epithrive® arm" — not "Epithrive® healed wounds 72% faster". The distinction matters for nutraceutical claim compliance.
14
Is there any RCT evidence — not just observational or open-label?
›

Answer: The Epithrive® Indian study is prospective and comparative, though open-label. For the formulation class (Arginine + Glutamine + HMB), there are published RCTs supporting the individual mechanisms and combinations:

  • Williams & Barbul 2002 (Annals of Surgery): Double-blind, randomised — Arg+HMB+Gln significantly increased wound collagen deposition in elderly volunteers.
  • Cereda et al. 2015: RCT — arginine-containing formula produced 19% greater wound area reduction in pressure ulcer patients vs. standard ONS.
  • Tatti et al. 2012: Arg+Gln+HMB blend reduced DFU healing time from 247 days to 83 days in a controlled study.
  • Nutrients Meta-Analysis (Arribas-López 2021): Systematic review of 44 human studies on Arginine and Glutamine supplementation — significant effects confirmed on healing parameters.

The global evidence base for this formulation class is robust. The Epithrive® Indian study adds localised, population-relevant data to this body of evidence.

15
How quickly should I expect to see results in my patients?
›

Answer: Based on clinical evidence and case data, typical observed progression in patients who respond:

  • Week 1–2: Reduced exudate, less peri-wound inflammation, improved wound bed colour (moving from pale to red/pink). These are early signs that perfusion and immune function are improving.
  • Week 2–4: Visible granulation tissue formation. Wound margins begin contracting. Slough reduction.
  • Week 4–8: Measurable reduction in wound area. In the Indian clinical study, 72.4% mean wound area reduction was observed at Week 8.

The 15-day diabetic case study showed near-complete epithelialization in an unusually fast timeline — this reflects both the formulation's efficacy and the importance of adequate local care alongside nutrition support.

Healing timelines vary based on wound severity, baseline nutrition, blood glucose control, and adequacy of local care. Never promise a specific timeline to patients or families.

Compliant framing: "Based on clinical study data, patients in the Epithrive® arm showed 72.4% wound area reduction at Week 8" — not "your patient will heal in 8 weeks".
16
Does nutrition actually make a meaningful difference, or is good wound dressing enough?
›

Answer: This is the most important conceptual question — and the answer is definitive. Wound healing is a systemic biological process, not a local one. The wound dressing manages the local environment: moisture balance, infection control, debridement. But the actual repair — angiogenesis, collagen synthesis, granulation tissue formation, re-epithelialization — is driven by systemic biology. That biology runs on substrates.

Imagine building a house. The dressing is the scaffolding and the safety net. But bricks, cement, wiring, and plumbing — the actual materials — have to come from somewhere. If the supply chain is cut off, the scaffolding stays up but the house never gets built. Nutrition is the supply chain.

30–50% of surgical and elderly patients in Indian hospitals are malnourished. In these patients, the body simply does not have enough Arginine, Glutamine, or collagen precursors to run the healing cascade effectively. No dressing — however advanced — can compensate for the absence of these substrates.

The clinical evidence confirms this. In the Epithrive® Indian study, both groups received identical standard wound care. The only difference was Epithrive® — and the difference in outcome was 20.3 percentage points.

What types of wounds respond best? Is it only for diabetic foot ulcers?
›

Answer: Epithrive® is not limited to DFU. The formulation supports wound healing through systemic nutritional mechanisms that are applicable across all wound types where nutritional depletion or metabolic stress impairs healing. Validated and appropriate indications:

  • Diabetic Foot Ulcers (Wagner 1–3) — primary indication; strongest evidence base
  • Pressure Injuries (Stage II–IV) — excellent evidence from Cereda et al. RCT and global literature
  • Post-Surgical Wounds — especially dehisced wounds, high-risk closures, post-debridement
  • Burns — Glutamine and Arginine are particularly important in burn patients (hypermetabolic state)
  • Venous Leg Ulcers — respond well to improved microcirculation (Arginine) and collagen support
  • Chronic non-healing wounds (>2 weeks) — any wound that has stalled regardless of aetiology
  • Traumatic wounds with tissue loss — significant ECM requirement

The SNAP Rule applies universally — any wound patient who is nutritionally compromised, or whose wound has stalled, is a candidate.

D — Practical & Usage Questions
18
How long should patients take Epithrive®? When do they stop?
›

Answer: The minimum therapeutic duration is 4–6 weeks. Wound healing biology takes time — a 2-week course will not complete the collagen remodeling phase. The correct answer is: continue until wound closure.

For practical guidance:

  • Mild-moderate wounds: typically 4–6 weeks (1 box minimum, likely 2–3 boxes)
  • Stage III–IV pressure injuries or Wagner 3 DFU: 8–12 weeks or more
  • Post-surgical wounds: 4 weeks minimum from surgery date

There is no harm in continued use beyond wound closure in malnourished patients — it supports systemic nutritional recovery. But the clinical indication ends at wound closure.

When should patients take it — morning, evening, with food, without food?
›

  • Any time of day — morning, afternoon, or evening are all appropriate
  • Between meals is slightly preferred — avoids competition with dietary protein for amino acid transporters, maximising amino acid absorption

My patient is already taking a multivitamin. Will there be any overdose risk from the micronutrients?
›

Answer: The micronutrient doses in Epithrive® are calibrated at therapeutic (not megadose) levels. For most patients taking a standard multivitamin, combined intake will remain within safe upper limits:

  • Zinc 13mg — Upper tolerable limit is 40mg/day. Combined with a standard MV (typically 5–10mg), total is 18–23mg — well within safe range
  • Vitamin A 750mcg — Upper limit is 3000mcg/day. Well within safe range even with a multivitamin
  • Vitamin E 7.5mg — Clinically significant effects start above 400mg; 7.5mg is negligible
  • Selenium 30mcg — Upper limit 400mcg; well within range

The only meaningful consideration is Vitamin A in patients already on high-dose retinol supplements (above 1500mcg/day from all sources). Ask patients about their supplement regimen at initiation.

⚖️ Regulatory Guardrails — Non-Negotiable

You CAN say
  • "Supports wound healing as an adjunct to standard wound care"
  • "Provides nutritional support for tissue repair"
  • "Clinical study (CSR-ETH-2025-001) showed 72.4% wound area reduction at Week 8"

  • "India's first targeted wound healing nutrition formula"
  • "Nutraceutical" or "specialized wound nutrition"
You CANNOT say
  • "Epithrive® treats / cures / heals wounds"
  • "FSMP" or "Food for Special Medical Purposes"
  • "Guaranteed results in X days"
  • "This is a medicine"
  • "Epithrive® will close the wound"
  • Any disease treatment claim
Mandatory disclaimer on all communications: "Dietary supplement. Not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
