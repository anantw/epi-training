Why Compliance Is Non-Negotiable

The stakes — what happens when things go wrong
⚠️

This Module Carries Legal Weight

Making an unlawful claim about a food or nutraceutical product in India is a violation of the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006 (FSSAI). Penalties include product seizure, licence suspension, fines, and in serious cases criminal prosecution. As an MR, you are the human interface between the product and the market. What you say in a doctor's chamber, in a WhatsApp message, or in printed materials is your personal responsibility — and the company's legal exposure.

🏛️

Regulatory Risk

FSSAI can issue notices, demand product recall, suspend licences, and impose fines if prohibited claims are found in marketing materials or sales presentations.

👤

Personal Liability

MRs who make prohibited claims are not shielded by the company — they bear personal responsibility. WhatsApp messages, printed materials, and verbal statements can all be used as evidence.

🤝

Doctor Trust

Overclaiming destroys credibility. A doctor who hears you say "Epithrive® heals wounds" immediately knows you're making an illegal claim — and questions your credibility on everything else.

The right framing: Compliance is not a constraint on your effectiveness — it is your protection. An MR who communicates within compliance rules is an MR who can be trusted, given more responsibility, and who builds a sustainable field career. Non-compliant MRs become liabilities.

The FSSAI Regulatory Framework

Three categories — and which one governs Epithrive® right now

India's Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) governs all food and nutrition products. Understanding where Epithrive® sits in the regulatory landscape is essential — it determines what claims are legally permissible. Three categories are relevant to Epithrive®.

Nutraceutical / Food Supplement

Category 13.6 · Current status

  • Regulated under FSSAI Nutraceuticals Regulations
  • No prescription required
  • May make nutritional/function claims about ingredients
  • Cannot make disease treatment/prevention claims
  • Mandatory disclaimer: "Not a drug / Dietary supplement"
  • Must not imply therapeutic equivalence to medicines
  • Can reference clinical evidence framed as nutritional outcomes
✓ EPITHRIVE® IS HERE NOW

Food for Special Medical Purposes (FSMP)

Pending FSSAI approval

  • Regulated under FSSR — FSMP Regulations
  • Higher standard for manufacturing, clinical evidence
  • Permits condition-specific nutritional claims (e.g., "for dietary management of wound healing")
  • Still NOT a medicine — cannot claim "treats" or "cures"
  • May be recommended by HCPs for specific conditions
  • Typically used under medical supervision
  • Requires FSSAI FSMP-specific approval — separate from nutraceutical
⏳ DO NOT USE "FSMP" YET

Drug / Pharmaceutical

Requires CDSCO approval

  • Regulated by CDSCO under Drugs and Cosmetics Act
  • Can legally claim to "treat", "cure", or "prevent" diseases
  • Requires prescription in most cases
  • Clinical trial requirements are far more rigorous
  • Manufacturing under drug GMP standards
  • Epithrive® is NOT a drug and will never use drug-level claims
  • Using drug-level claims for a nutraceutical is a serious regulatory violation
✗ NOT APPLICABLE TO EPITHRIVE®
The key implication for MRs: Because Epithrive® is a nutraceutical, it operates between "general food" and "drug" in the regulatory hierarchy. This means you can reference clinical evidence and nutritional mechanisms — but you cannot say the product treats, cures, or prevents any disease. The language you use must always frame Epithrive® as providing nutritional support, not medical treatment.

Phrase-by-Phrase Compliance Guide

Every critical claim — the wrong version and the right version, side by side

These are the specific phrases that come up in field conversations. Memorise the compliant versions until they come out automatically. The non-compliant versions in red are what your instincts might want to say — the compliant versions in green are what you must say.

The Core Product Claim
✗ Non-Compliant
"Epithrive® heals diabetic wounds and bedsores."
Prohibited: makes a disease treatment claim. "Heals" is a drug-level action claim.
✓ Compliant
"Epithrive® provides targeted nutritional support for wound healing as an adjunct to standard wound care."
Correct: frames the product as nutritional support, not a treatment.
Presenting the Clinical Study Finding
✗ Non-Compliant
"Epithrive® is proven to heal wounds 39% faster."
Prohibited: "proven to heal" is a drug-level efficacy claim. No attribution to specific study.
✓ Compliant
"In our clinical study (CSR-ETH-2025-001), the Epithrive® arm showed 72.4% wound area reduction at Week 8 versus 52.1% in the standard care arm — p=0.004."
Correct: cites the specific study, frames as study finding not a product claim, attributable.
Referring to the Regulatory Category
✗ Non-Compliant
"This is an FSMP — a Food for Special Medical Purposes approved for wound patients."
Prohibited: FSMP approval not yet granted. Never use until official notice received.
✓ Compliant
"Epithrive® is a nutraceutical registered under FSSAI (Licence No. 11524998000214) — a dietary supplement formulated specifically to provide wound healing nutritional support."
Correct: accurate regulatory designation, no FSMP claim.
Presenting the Healing Timeframe
✗ Non-Compliant
"Your patient's wound will close in 15 days on Epithrive®."
Prohibited: guaranteed outcome claim. You cannot guarantee individual patient results.
✓ Compliant
"We have a documented case where a diabetic patient with a chronic ulcer experienced near-complete closure in 15 days alongside standard care. Individual outcomes vary based on wound type, baseline nutrition, and comorbidities."
Correct: case study framed as individual documentation with appropriate caveat.
Infection and Complication Reduction
✗ Non-Compliant
"Epithrive® prevents wound infection and reduces the need for antibiotics."
Prohibited: disease prevention claim. Reduces antibiotic need is a drug-level claim.
✓ Compliant
"L-Glutamine supports immune cell function, which is essential for the body's natural defence in the wound environment. Wischmeyer (2003) found 45% fewer ICU infections with Glutamine supplementation."
Correct: mechanism claim for ingredient + attributed study finding, not a product claim.
Hospital Stay and Cost Savings
✗ Non-Compliant
"Epithrive® will reduce your patients' hospital stay by 3–4 days and save the hospital ₹15,000 per patient."
Prohibited: guaranteed outcome claim stated as a product promise without attribution.
✓ Compliant
"Williams and Barbul (2002, Annals of Surgery) found a 3.5-day reduction in hospital stay with Arg+HMB+Gln supplementation in trauma patients. Faster healing generally correlates with fewer dressing changes and shorter stays — the literature estimates ₹10,000–₹15,000 savings per patient."
Correct: attributed to literature, framed as general correlation not a guarantee.
Describing What the Product Is
✗ Non-Compliant
"Epithrive® is a medicine / drug / treatment for wound healing."
Prohibited: positions a nutraceutical as a pharmaceutical. Serious regulatory violation.
✓ Compliant
"Epithrive® is India's first targeted wound-healing nutrition formula — a dietary supplement that provides the specific amino acids, collagen peptides, and micronutrients the body needs to support the healing process alongside standard wound care."
Correct: clearly identifies as nutrition / dietary supplement, emphasises "alongside" standard care.
The mandatory disclaimer — say it or print it wherever outcomes are discussed: "Dietary supplement. Not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. For nutritional support as an adjunct to standard wound care." This single disclaimer correctly positions Epithrive® and provides legal protection for every outcome claim made in context.

Digital & Print Communication Rules

What you can send, share, post, and print — and what you cannot

Primary MR-to-doctor communication channel

Doctor summary, clinical dossier, case study cards

✓Only leave company-approved, officially printed materials — never self-printed unofficial sheets
✓Approved materials: Doctor A4 Summary, Formulation Explainer, Case Study Document, Clinical Study Report (on request)
✓Every piece of printed material must carry the mandatory disclaimer and FSSAI licence number
✗Do not write on approved materials or add handwritten outcome claims
✗Do not design or print your own one-pagers, posters, or data sheets — even if they look professional
✗Do not use any non-company branded materials in a doctor's office

Social Media (Personal Accounts)

Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X

✓Share official company-posted content from the Epithrive® or Nuin Wellness accounts — no modification
✓Tag Epithrive® in shares of approved educational content
✗Do not post patient photos, wound photos, or before/after images — privacy violation + potential regulatory issue
✗Do not make any product claims on your personal social accounts
✗Do not run paid ads for Epithrive® from personal accounts
✗Do not write posts like "Epithrive® saved my patient's leg" — this is both a prohibited claim and a patient confidentiality breach
🗣️

Verbal Communication (Doctor Calls)

In-clinic, phone calls, conferences, CME events

✓Use only compliant claim language — assume every conversation could be recorded
✓State the study finding precisely: "72.4% vs 52.1% at Week 8, p=0.004" — not paraphrased versions
✓Verbally include disclaimer when discussing any outcome: "As a dietary supplement, results vary by patient..."
✗Do not adjust your story when you think no one is listening — consistency is your protection
✗Do not make verbal promises about outcomes that differ from what the written materials say
✗Do not imply that the product has drug approval or is under CDSCO regulation

🚨 Mandatory Immediate Escalation
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis, throat swelling, breathing difficulty)
  • Patient hospitalisation attributable to the product
  • Death (any case where product use is in the history)
  • Unexpected serious adverse event not listed in known profile
  • Significant organ impairment (liver, kidney) with temporal link to product use
⚠️ Document & Escalate Within 48 Hours
  • Persistent GI discomfort beyond first week (bloating, nausea)
  • Metallic taste complained about for more than 5 days
  • Persistent flushing or headache beyond 3 days
  • Patient stops taking product due to a side effect
  • Any symptom the patient or doctor attributes to the product
Critical rule on adverse events: You are not authorised to tell a patient or doctor that a reported symptom is "not from Epithrive®" or to minimise a report. Document everything and escalate. Never advise a patient to continue taking the product after a serious adverse event — tell them to stop use and consult their doctor immediately while you escalate through the chain.

Ethics & HCP Interaction Rules

What you may and may not offer doctors — and how to build trust without crossing lines

✓ You MAY provide

  • Company-approved clinical detailing materials (printed)
  • Clinical Study Report (CSR) or doctor summary — on request
  • Formulation explainer document
  • Case study documentation (company printed)
  • Product samples authorised by your Area Manager
  • Factual answers to clinical questions (within your knowledge)
  • CME-related educational content when approved by company

✗ You may NOT provide

  • Cash payments of any kind — per-prescription incentives are illegal
  • Gifts of monetary value (above token — no electronics, travel, hospitality)
  • Promise of return business or referrals in exchange for recommendations
  • Misleading comparative data against competitors
  • Any information you know to be false or exaggerated
  • Product samples beyond authorised quantities
  • Clinical opinions beyond your role as MR (do not diagnose or advise clinical treatment)

△ Context-Dependent

  • Branded merchandise (pens, notepads) — permitted if nominal value, branded clearly, not payment for recommendation
  • Product-related meals during presentations — follow company policy; document
  • Educational event sponsorship — must go through company approval, never direct MR-funded
  • Responding to clinical questions outside your expertise — answer within known facts, escalate complex questions to Medical Affairs
The "would I be comfortable if my manager saw this?" test: Before giving anything to a doctor — a document, a statement, a gift, a promise — ask yourself if you would be comfortable with your Area Manager, and then the company's Medical Affairs team, seeing exactly what you did. If the answer is no — don't do it.

Escalation Protocol — Questions You Cannot Answer

Knowing the limits of your role is compliance. If a doctor asks a question you are not sure about — regulatory, clinical, or legal — the correct answer is to escalate, not guess.

Clinical Question

"Can I give this to my Stage 5 CKD patient?" → Say: "Let me confirm this with our Medical Affairs team and get back to you today." Escalate to AM → Medical Affairs → response to doctor.

Regulatory Question

"Can you list this on your hospital formulary?" → Say: "That's something our institutional team handles — let me connect you with the right person." Do not quote process you don't know.

Adverse Event Question

"My patient had X after starting Epithrive®" → Document immediately. Do NOT diagnose or minimise. Say: "Thank you for telling me — I'll document this and escalate to our medical team today."
